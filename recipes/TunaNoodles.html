Campbell's Tuna Noodle Casserole

Description

This tuna, noodles, pickles, and cheese recipe came from my grandmother, who used peas instead of pickles in her version. My mother changed the recipe, and I have altered it even more. The flavors of these ingredients blend together in what is reminiscent of a cold macaroni or potato salad. It will be your family's new favorite!

Ingredients

  • 8 ounces uncooked elbow macaroni
  • 6 ounces Colby-Jack cheese, cubed
  • 2 dill pickles, chopped
  • 1 (5 ounce) can albacore tuna in water, drained and flaked
  • ½ cup light mayonnaise
  • ½ teaspoon prepared yellow mustard
  • 1 teaspoon dill pickle juice
  • salt and ground black pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Bring a saucepan of lightly salted water to a boil. Cook elbow macaroni in the boiling water, stirring occasionally, until tender yet firm to the bite, about 7 minutes. Rinse under cold running water, then drain well and pat lightly with paper towels.

  2. Combine macaroni, Colby-Jack cheese, pickles, tuna, mayonnaise, and mustard in a large bowl. Season with pickle juice, salt, and black pepper.

  3. Cover bowl; refrigerate for at least 30 minutes before serving.
